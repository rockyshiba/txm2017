These berries created a hype unlike any other superfood because they’re high in antioxidants, omega fatty acids (yes, a fat that’s good for you does actually exist), protein, and fibre.

The acai berry grows in South American rainforests and has been shown to have twice the antioxidants found in blueberries, as well as omega fatty acids, protein, and fibre. It's so powerful, some studies have shown it kills off cancer cells in a lab setting.

Found in China, Mongolia, and in the Tibetan Himalayas, goji berries have been used for 6,000 years by herbalists to protect the liver, help eyesight, improve sexual function and fertility, boost immune function, improve circulation, and promote longevity.

While more conclusive research is needed, daily goji berry consumption for a two-week period is associated with feelings of well-being and improving digestive health.

Kiwi fruit contains high amounts of vitamin C (even more than oranges) and as much potassium as bananas. Kiwis also work as a digestive aid and help maintain healthy skin and hair. Originally known as "Chinese gooseberries" this Asian fruit assumed a new alias when New Zealand began growing and exporting them in the 60s.

Kiwis actually contain more Vitamin C than most citrus fruits, sometimes nearly twice the amount. Vitamin C keeps your immune system healthy.

50% of the Asian dragonfruit's seeds are made up of essential oleic acid, which helps lower bad cholesterol and raise good cholesterol. The fruit is obtained from succulent stems of several cactus species native to Thailand and Vietnam.

Dragon fruit is a source of fibre. Fibre lowers your cholesterol and maintains your blood sugar. Dragon fruit is the fertilized flowers of the Mexican pitahaya cactus.

The mangosteen, native to Southeast Asia, is a rich source of antioxidants and contains chemicals called xanthones, which appear to have potent anti-inflammatory effects in the laboratory.

Mangosteen skin is rich in xanthones which provide a wide variety of disease-fighting properties (think anti-bacterial, antiviral, and antioxidant.)
