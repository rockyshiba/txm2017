Tea

There are more than 1,500 kinds of tea from all around the world. Tea is a natural way to stay hydrated. In fact drinking a hot cup of tea can help you relax and reduce stress.

Every day scientists are trying to understand how the antioxidants in tea impact the human body, but for now, drink up it's natural flavour.

Chamomile Tea

“Chamomile tea is associated with an increase of glycine, a chemical that relaxes nerves and muscles and acts like a mild sedative.”

Green Tea

“Two green tea compounds, L-thenanine and caffeine ,can significantly boost levels of attention and alertness. Green tea is less likely to make you jittery and anxious than other energy-boosting drinks because it contains lower levels of caffeine than other teas or coffee.”

Matcha

"Matcha tea benefits are 10 to 15 times higher in nutrients than regular teas. Matcha green tea is a powerhouse of antioxidants. Matcha tea has three times more caffeine than green tea or the equivalent of drinking one cup of coffee, but you don’t get the same buzz because it contains L-theanine, a compound known to induce relaxation without drowsiness."

Peppermint

“Peppermint tea reduces stress and relieves you from anxiety. The menthol contained in peppermint is a natural muscle relaxant. When you’re stressed out or anxious, you tend to feel very tense and irritable. Drinking peppermint tea will help you have an overall calm.”

Bergamot

“The bergamot orange contained in earl grey tea is particularly effective at preventing colds and the onset of the flu. The antioxidants in bergamot fight against infections, fever, and other chronic ailments.”
